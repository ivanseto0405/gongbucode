Hangul

In English writing we use the alphabet or roman letters. But in Korean, a separate Korean alphabet or letter called han-geul [한글] was created by King Sejong around 1443.

Just as in the Roman alphabet, in the han-geul alphabetical arrangement also consists of vowels and consonants. If in the Roman alphabet we know 5 vowels, namely a, e, i, o and u, and 21 consonants, but in hangeul there are 10 basic vowels and 14 basic consonants.

The Hangeul letter has 24 basic characters, consisting of 10 vowels and 14 consonant letters. Then from those characters, 11 vowels and 16 double consonants were added.
